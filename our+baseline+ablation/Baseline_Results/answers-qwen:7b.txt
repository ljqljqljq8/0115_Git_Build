In network protocols, Link-State (LS) and Distance-Vector (DV) are two fundamental routing algorithms.

1. **Distance-Vector (DV)**: This algorithm works by exchanging route information directly between neighboring routers. Each router maintains a list of known routes and their distances from the router. When a change in route occurs, routers disseminate this information to their neighbors. The final route is computed by each device based on the shortest path found.

2. **Link-State (LS)****: In contrast, the LS algorithm collects information about the entire network, including links between routers and potential forwarding paths. This information is then translated into a network-wide routing table that is distributed to all routers in the system.

Thus, the key difference between the two algorithms lies in their reliance on information exchange. TheDValgorithm relies on direct communication between neighbors, whileLSalgorithm collects global information and distributes it locally.
